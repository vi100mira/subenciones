# Third-Sector Product Principles

## Definition

This product is built for third-sector and social-impact entities: private organizations whose primary purpose is social, community, cultural, environmental, or solidarity impact rather than ordinary profit maximization.

Examples include NGOs, foundations, associations, social cooperatives, insertion entities, community organizations, federations, and other mission-led entities.

## Why It Matters

Third-sector entities can have revenue, staff, budgets, public contracts, grants, donations, and paid services. The difference is that economic activity supports a mission. The product must therefore optimize for trust, sustainability, transparency, and social value, not extractive growth.

This is not a generic commercial CRM or grant-writing tool. It is a privacy-first grants intelligence cockpit for organizations that often work with vulnerable people, public funds, mixed financing, and strict accountability duties.

## Product Consequences

- Public grant discovery should remain low-friction and not be artificially scarce.
- Paid value should come from operational support: team workspace, alerts, private source connectors, review workflows, Word drafting, audit, and governance.
- Pricing should be socially proportionate, with room for small-entity tiers, solidarity plans, or sponsored access.
- The product must never monetize sensitive beneficiary data, private embeddings, or paid placement in opportunity rankings.
- AI recommendations are advisory and explainable, not automatic eligibility decisions.
- Human review is required before exports, submissions, channel sends, or reuse of generated content.

## Data Consequences

Third-sector work can involve special-category or high-risk data: disability, health, migration status, minors, violence, poverty, exclusion, employment vulnerability, or personal intervention histories.

The default posture is therefore:

- collect the minimum data needed
- ask consent before public-web analysis or private connectors
- separate public grant retrieval from tenant-private retrieval
- treat private embeddings as tenant-private data
- block sensitive beneficiary records from the MVP
- show evidence, provenance, data class, and review status

## Business Red Lines

- Do not sell, reuse, or train on tenant-private data.
- Do not convert vulnerability into a commercial asset.
- Do not charge for rights such as consent, deletion, export of own data, or account closure.
- Do not rank grants by paid placement.
- Do not hide official public information behind artificial scarcity.
- Do not remove human accountability from funding decisions.
